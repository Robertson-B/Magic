Running a Commander Tournament

This guide covers using {{ site_name }} to manage a 4-round Swiss commander tournament with 45-minute round windows.

Before the tournament

  1. Gather player names — Get a list of all participating players
  2. Create a tournament — Go to the home page, enter tournament name, player count, and names
  3. The app builds pods — Round 1 is randomized; rounds 2-4 seed by wins and opponent strength
  4. Print or reference pod assignments — Round 1 is locked in, but rounds 2-4 will update after scoring

Running each round (45 minutes)

  • Announce pods — Call out which players are in each pod (1-4 players per pod)
  • Start the clock — Players have 45 minutes to play their game
  • Record final positions — After time, note each player's final score or position (highest to lowest)
  • Enter into app — Click the tournament in History, go to current round, enter each player's score
  • Save scores — Click "Save round scores" to calculate advancement

The 4-round format

Round 1 (Random): Players are randomized into 4-player pods.

Rounds 2-3 (Swiss seeding): Players ranked by wins → opponent strength → points. Top players are paired first.

Round 4 (Finals): The top 4 players automatically play in Pod 1 (clearly labeled "FINALS"). Everyone else also plays their own pods.

Scoring system

Each pod, players are ranked by final score (highest to lowest):

  • 1st place: 3 points + 1 win recorded
  • 2nd place: 2 points
  • 3rd place: 1 point
  • 4th place: 0 points

Note: Wins are the primary ranking metric. Points and opponent strength are tiebreakers.

After round 4

Once all 4 rounds are scored, the tournament is marked complete. The player with the most wins (and best tiebreakers) is crowned champion. A confetti effect celebrates on the detail page!

All tournaments are saved permanently in the History section.

Tips for running smooth rounds

  • Announce round and time limit clearly before starting
  • Use a timer or have someone track the 45 minutes
  • Be ready to record scores quickly after each round ends
  • Check the app after round 4 to see the finals pod clearly marked
  • Use History to review completed tournaments anytime
